# Proof-of-Concept Strategy

## Problem Statement

[Client] has a well-established [business type] but the technical infrastructure is a desert — no marketing CRM, no lead gen system, no automated follow-ups. Previous agencies failed to deliver. They need a complete technical foundation before scaling.

## Demand Evidence

- **Client IS the paying customer** — they've spent real money on marketing agencies and in-house experts
- **Insider credibility** — years of industry experience means peers trust their recommendations
- **Market is massive** — thousands of similar businesses with the same tech gap
- **Named ROI** — clear revenue impact of even basic improvements

## Status Quo (What most businesses in this space do)
- Legacy tools for production — no marketing CRM
- No lead capture, no nurturing, no automated follow-up
- Manual review of every lead/claim/candidate
- Referral partners managed via ad-hoc calls and texts
- Agencies hired, fail to deliver, trust destroyed

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Primary:** Mid-size operators ($3-10M/year) with office staff drowning in manual work
**Secondary:** Large operators ($10M+) and Private Equity groups

**The real moat is not the tech — it's the client's industry credibility.** Any agency can build a CRM. Few have the domain expertise and peer trust.

**Wedge:** *"Here's my company running on this system — here's my dashboard, here's my lead flow. I was you. Let me do it for you."*

## Approach: Full Proof-of-Concept

Build the complete system on the client's own company over 8-12 weeks. Then use it as a showcase to sell to peers.

## Existing Infrastructure Advantage

- AI agent infrastructure — control room + VPS + monitoring
- Domain-specific automation (already built for similar use cases)
- Competitive intelligence system
- CRM + bot expertise
- Multi-company brain — when sister companies launch, they connect to the same system
